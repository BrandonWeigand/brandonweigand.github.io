    Professionals achieving an "A" rank in a specific technology are experts in wielding the associated tool. They possess the competence to execute any task within the technological domain, showcasing mastery in its application.
  3. B (80-89)%
    Professionals attaining a "B" rank in a specific technology demonstrate the capability to accomplish nearly any task within the technology's parameters.   4. C (70-79)%
    Professionals attaining a "C" rank in a specific technology can effectively complete most tasks with minimal reliance on advice from internet forums or more experienced professionals.
  5. D (65-69)%
    Professionals achieving a "D" rank in a specific technology have fulfilled the minimum skill requirements to be considered professionals. They can successfully accomplish most tasks; however, they may find it necessary to seek frequent advice from internet forums and more experienced professionals.
  6. F (0-64)%
    Individuals holding an "F" rank in a particular technology lack the capability to operate the corresponding tool at a professional level.
Why letter grading over seniority labels?
I have chosen to refrain from utilizing non-specific seniority-based terms such as "Senior" or "Junior" in favor of implementing a skill-based letter grading system. This approach mitigates instances where one might assume someone is a proficient PHP developer, only to discover that their expertise lies in 12 years of experience with Assembly language.
